What are Olympiads?

Academic olympiads are international competitions testing excellence in specific subjects — Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Informatics, and Astronomy among others. Students typically qualify through national-level rounds before representing their country globally.

Olympiad medals are among the most recognized credentials in academia. IMO gold medalists, for instance, are recruited directly by top universities worldwide without traditional admission requirements.

Major Olympiad Subjects

∑

Mathematics (IMO)

The International Mathematical Olympiad — held since 1959. 6 problems over 2 days. The most prestigious math competition in the world.

⚛

Physics (IPhO)

International Physics Olympiad tests theoretical and experimental physics. Hosted annually in a different country.

⚗

Chemistry (IChO)

International Chemistry Olympiad. Theoretical and practical rounds testing deep chemistry knowledge.

🧬

Biology (IBO)

International Biology Olympiad covers molecular biology, genetics, ecology, anatomy, and physiology.

💻

Informatics (IOI)

International Olympiad in Informatics — algorithmic problem solving. Two days, 3 tasks per day.

🌌

Astronomy (IOAA)

International Olympiad on Astronomy and Astrophysics covering theory, data analysis, and observation rounds.
