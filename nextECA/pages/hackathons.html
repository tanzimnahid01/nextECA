What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is an intensive event — typically 24 to 72 hours — where teams collaborate to build a software, hardware, or creative project from scratch. They're one of the best ways to learn fast, meet like-minded peers, and build your portfolio. Many startups and careers have launched from hackathon projects.

Hackathons come in many forms: online/offline, solo/team, theme-specific (sustainability, AI, health), or open-ended. Most are free to enter. Prize pools range from recognition certificates to $100,000+.

Tips for First-Timers

01

Find Your Niche

Look for hackathons themed around your interest — AI, healthcare, fintech, environment. Passion drives better projects.

02

Build a Balanced Team

Combine coders, designers, and presenters. A team with diverse skills wins more consistently than all-coders.

03

Done > Perfect

Ship a working MVP. Judges want to see something functional and clearly communicated, not a half-finished masterpiece.

04

Tell a Story

Your pitch matters as much as your code. Practice your demo. Clearly state the problem, solution, and impact.
